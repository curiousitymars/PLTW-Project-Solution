Inflow Pipes

The purpose of the inflow pipes are to draw in the water, thus beginning the process. The idea is that if the inflow pipes draw from overground rivers and lakes which are most prone to flooding rather than underground springs of aquifers. This way, not only will the flood water be transported away but also repurposed to provide water to those in need. And the fact that it is not overground reduces the risk of displacement and problems with the ecosystem.

Sedimentation Basin

The purpose of the Sedimentation Basin is normally to flush out any sediments, thus beginning the filtration process. However, since we are withdrawing the water from the body of water itself rather than a spring or aquifer, it will make the water more valuable, therefore, using water to flush out the sediments would reduce the water quantity.The idea of this part of the solution is to directly deposit the sediments into the underground earth, allowing only the water to pass into the next step.

Deposit Space Maker

The purpose of the deposit space maker is to make space for the deposited sediments. The idea is, as stated before, to directly release the sediments underground. So, if the depositing space gest filled, the space maker will dig in order to fit the sediments.

Filtration

The purpose of the filtration is to filter the drawn out flood water, thus repurposing it and using it to serve the community.

Crude Water Outflow

The purpose of the crude water outflow is to release the very little unclean water remaining, rather than carrying it all the way to the final path seperation and releasing it from there. The use of this would also be to release any water clogging up the filter.

Final Outflow Pipes

The purpose of the final outflow pipes is to send the water its way, making room for more. This water will be used overground by the members of the community, allowing the process to begin once more.
